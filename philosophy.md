# Training Philosophy

## Current Goals
- Primary: Build muscle and strength
- Secondary: Aesthetics / body composition; joint health and longevity

## Training Frequency & Split
- Days per week: 3–4
- Split: Push / Pull / Legs — cycled continuously, never repeating a session before completing a full PPL cycle
- Rest day rules: No specific rules; rest days fall naturally around the cycle

## Programming Preferences
- Rep ranges for main lifts: 4–6 reps (first, heaviest exercise per muscle group)
- Rep ranges for accessories: 4–8 reps (some variance acceptable, not deliberately exceeded)
- Progression method: Add weight when the top of the rep range is hit across all sets
- AMRAP sets: No

## Exercise Preferences
- Main lifts: See exercise database (not enumerated here)
- Preferred accessories: See exercise database
- Avoid: Unspecific compound movements chosen for "stability" or "functional strength"; exercises that cannot be justified by clear mechanical tension on the target muscle. Compounds are acceptable when the target muscle is the clear limiting factor.

## Session Structure
- 2 exercises per muscle group per session
- Per muscle group: 2 × 2–3 sets per exercise (4–6 working sets total per muscle group)
- A full Push day typically totals ~6 exercises and 15–18 working sets across chest, shoulders, and triceps
- Max session length: 90 minutes

## Core Training Philosophy
- Exercise selection is driven by mechanical tension on the target muscle — choose exercises that place the most effective load through the muscle's range of motion
- Pair exercises within a muscle group to cover different angles or emphases (e.g. incline + flat for chest, long-head + lateral-head for triceps), while recognising this is a quality-of-life preference, not a hard rule
- Grip and angle variation is welcome but not dogmatically required (e.g. two supinated curl variations is fine — a hammer curl is not mandatory)
- No PT buzzwords: avoid "functional strength", "muscle balance", "stabiliser work", or similar non-mechanistic framing
- All programming decisions should have a clear, evidence-based rationale rooted in muscle physiology and biomechanics

## Deload Policy
- No planned deloads

## Equipment
- Commercial gym with full rack, cables, machines (pin-loaded and plate-loaded), dumbbells, smith machine

## Current Weak Points / Focus Areas
- None identified

## Notes for Workout Planning
- When generating sessions, respect the continuous PPL cycle — track which session type was last completed and serve the next one in sequence
- Do not suggest exercises outside the exercise database without explicit user approval
- Do not add volume beyond the 2-exercise, 2×2–3 set structure without being asked
- Avoid framing exercise choices in aesthetic or motivational language; explain selections in terms of mechanics and evidence
